Creative Brief — Section Art

Echoes in the Machine — Chapters 7–9

```

The Assignment

One full-page interior illustration. First-person POV through Amara’s AR glasses, moments after she wakes and puts them on. The reader is inside her eyes. The digital world has already decided to be there too.

Aesthetic: cyberpunk psychological horror. Intimate. Invasive. The feeling of waking from a nightmare and discovering the nightmare has followed you into the room.

The Image

Foreground — The HUD

Thin, sleek icy-blue digital framing lines at the edges of the frame establish that we are looking through AR lenses. This layer is razor-sharp. The centre of the vision is dominated by the system override. Nothing about the interface looks damaged or glitching — it looks perfectly functional. That is the problem.

Midground — The Physical Reaction

At the very bottom edge of the frame, slightly out of focus: Amara’s bare hands gripping dark bedsheets. Knuckles stark white. The physiological spike is visible in the grip. She is not moving. She is reading.

Background — The Domestic Void

The dark, impeccably clean, minimalist lines of her Smart Apartment. Through floor-to-ceiling windows: the CN Tower, illuminated against the Toronto night sky. Cold. Distant. Completely indifferent to what is happening in the room.

The Text — The Breach

Hovering perfectly centred in the visual field, in the harsh jagged 7-segment red digital clock typeface — the same domestic-retro font that has no business being inside a piece of government-grade AR hardware:

THE JACARANDA WAS REAL. ASMARA.
ERIKSON CHECKED YOUR HARNESS TWICE.

This font is the violation. It should look like an analog infection bleeding into her sleek, ultra-modern interface. Not a system error. A message. Composed. Deliberate. Waiting for her to wake up.

// CLASSIFICATION UPGRADE: SPECTER
// ENCRYPTION BYPASSED

Corner readouts — small, standard HUD font, icy blue.

Colour Palette

  • The physical world: Deep midnight blues, slate greys, muted Toronto city-glow through the glass. Cold and isolating.
  • The digital intrusion: Arterial glowing red for the text. It casts a faint red ambient reflection onto Amara’s white-knuckled hands at the bottom of the frame — the digital light landing on skin. This grounds it.
  • The sensory ghost: At the absolute edges of the lenses — almost imperceptible — a faint haze of pale jacaranda-purple light. The olfactory memory of the dream bleeding into the visual field. It should not be obvious. It should be the thing the viewer notices second.
  • HUD elements: Icy blue, thin, clinical

Tone Reference

The three Echoes section covers form a triptych. Ch. 1–4: a human eye becoming a machine. Ch. 5–6: a machine eye encountering a human moment it cannot classify. Ch. 7–9: a human looking through a machine that has already been compromised, reading a message that should not exist, written in a font that belongs on a night table.

The red text is not a threat. It is a proof. The image should make the reader feel the difference.
```
