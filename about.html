FROM THE EDITOR'S DESK

July 14, 19XX

You were not found by accident…

The industry has become very good at surfaces.

The work I brought to them was too loud, too jagged, too insistent on making the reader feel something that didn’t have a market category. I sat in offices while “they” explained what readers wanted, but what “they” meant was what readers had already bought.

Just a machine that synthesizes the past, ready to print and re-print.

The fiction I wanted moved fast and left marks. The art I wanted refused to sit quietly on the page. Strange, specific, and uncomfortable. The kind of work that used to get printed on paper that yellowed before the decade was out, paid poorly and largely forgotten. But the ink smeared on your thumbs and you knew a hand had put it there.

I started Astounding Tales because the thing I actually wanted to make had been made unavailable through official channels.

The first issue is called Signal and Noise. The writing is finished. What you are holding right now is not a pitch. It is an inventory of what exists and what is still missing. I have marked the gaps clearly.

I am not asking you to fill all of them. I am asking you to find the one that belongs to you.

If it interests you, I would like to hear from you.

— Reginald Hollis
Editor, Astounding Tales
